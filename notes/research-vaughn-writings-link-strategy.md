## Stable URL Set

- https://vaughntan.org/notknowing  
  Best main target. Public hub for the not-knowing project. It frames four types of non-risk not-knowing, explains why risk tools fail in genuinely uncertain situations, and collects the major essays and toolkit pieces.

- https://vaughntan.org/nksynthesis/  
  Best specific synthesis target if the page needs a shorter, more direct "read one thing" link. It summarizes the not-knowing framework and its implications for decision-making.

- https://vaughntan.org/generative-uncertainty  
  Best specific post for innovation and organization-design relevance. It argues that uncertainty can be designed to be generative, with guardrails, emergence, and flexible support.

- https://vaughntan.org/desperationdesign  
  Best specific post for productive discomfort and team adaptation. It connects innovation work to discomfort from not knowing how to do the work yet, and discusses deliberate organizational design for learning, role flexibility, and new work patterns.

- https://vaughntan.org/learnwhatmatters  
  Best specific post for work patterns and tacit knowledge. It argues that organizations teach hard-to-write-down knowledge by making work concrete and public early, focusing feedback on outcomes, and using concrete examples.

- https://vaughntan.org/newsletter  
  Useful secondary "more writing" target. It describes the Uncertainty Mindset newsletter and links to the annotated first-year archive, but is weaker as the primary credibility link.

## Sampled Findings

The current through-line across the sampled writing is not merely "uncertainty" as a topic. It is a practical design stance: distinguish types of not-knowing, avoid treating every unknown as quantifiable risk, and build organizational habits that keep teams adaptive when work is genuinely new.

The not-knowing hub is the best bridge to WorkGraph because it is organized around diagnosis and response: what kind of incomplete knowledge is present, what tools fit that kind, and why explicit language improves decisions. This maps well to WorkGraph's visible computation graphs and human-agent coordination.

The organization-design pieces add the founder-card texture. "Generative uncertainty" is the strongest conceptual article for innovation. "Desperation by design" is the strongest article for productive discomfort and team adaptation. "Learning what matters" is the strongest article for concrete work patterns because it emphasizes public early prototypes, outcome-focused feedback, and shared examples.
