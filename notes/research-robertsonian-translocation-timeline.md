# Robertsonian Translocation Timeline

## Key Facts

- W. R. B. Robertson's original paper is:
  Robertson, Wm. Rees B. "Chromosome studies. I. Taxonomic relationships shown in the chromosomes of Tettigidae and Acrididae: V-shaped chromosomes and their significance in Acrididae, Locustidae, and Gryllidae: Chromosomes and variation." Journal of Morphology 27(2):179-331 (June 1916). DOI: https://doi.org/10.1002/jmor.1050270202
- The 2025 mechanism paper is:
  de Lima, L. G., Guarracino, A., Koren, S. et al. "The formation and propagation of human Robertsonian chromosomes." Nature 647:952-961. Published 24 September 2025; version of record 24 September 2025. DOI: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-025-09540-8
- Erik Garrison is listed as an author on the Nature paper, affiliated with the Department of Genetics, Genomics and Informatics, University of Tennessee Health Science Center.

## Interval

- Conservative statement: Robertsonian chromosomes were first recognized in 1916, and the Gomes de Lima / Guarracino / Koren et al. mechanism paper was published in 2025: 109 years later.
- More precise, with the available dates: Robertson's paper is dated only to the June 1916 issue, while the Nature paper was published on 24 September 2025. That is approximately 109 years and 3 months.
- If a nominal June date is needed, the elapsed interval to 24 September 2025 ranges from 109.24 to 109.32 tropical years depending on whether the June issue is treated as 30 June or 1 June. A mid-month convention gives 109.28 years. Use "109 years" or "more than a century" in public copy rather than a false exact day count.

## Source Notes

- Nature's article page states that Robertsonian chromosomes were "First recognized in 1916 by Robertson in grasshopper karyotypes" and cites Robertson as reference 6.
- The same Nature page cites Robertson's Journal of Morphology paper as volume 27, pages 179-331, year 1916, and gives the 2025 article citation, publication date, version-of-record date, and DOI.
- Crossref's Wiley metadata for the Robertson DOI gives issue 2, pages 179-331, and `published-print` as June 1916.
- The digitized Journal of Morphology volume 27 table of contents places Robertson's article in No. 2, June 1916. The scanned text includes "Journal of Morphology, Vol. 27, No. 2, June, 1916" on Robertson article pages.
