### Plaintext version (for reference)

Erik Garrison runs pseudo-homologous region discovery across vertebrate pangenomes through WorkGraph. Luca Pinello uses it for clinical genomics analysis in his lab. Vaughn Tan uses it as the substrate for his research on how hybrid human-AI teams actually function. They share one dependency graph. Specialist agents (literature, variant analysis, validation) claim tasks. The founders review, steer, hand off. Every claim, log, completion, and artifact is preserved.

WorkGraph is open source, written in Rust. The graph is JSONL in git, one task per line. A reader can replay any decision: when Erik prioritizes a candidate variant, the chain back to its evidence is browsable. When an agent fails, the trace shows what it tried, what it cost, and where the work resumed. The artifact is the work, not a summary of the work.

Poietic incorporated as a Delaware public-benefit corporation in April 2026, with the purpose written into the filing: **to make human and machine collaboration legible and responsive to its participants**. That incorporation was coordinated through WorkGraph. The trace is browsable at ulivo.poietic.life/wg/feeds/incorporation-trace/. It is not a polished demo, it is the actual graph, with artifacts and dead ends included.
