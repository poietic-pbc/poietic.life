# v3 spine brief: shared frame for fanout drafters

## 1. SPINE

Poietic PBC builds open-source infrastructure (WorkGraph) for reliable,
careful, auditable hybrid human-AI work in computational and clinical
genomics. The PBC mission, from the Delaware Certificate of Amendment,
is the spine: "The specific public benefit purpose of the Corporation
is to make human and machine collaboration legible and responsive to
its participants." Science is the proving ground, not the headline.
WorkGraph operationalizes the mission.

## 2. CONCRETE ANCHOR: REFERENCE RESOURCES

Erik's authoritative track record is building foundational reference
resources used by thousands of downstream labs: vg, PGGB, the HPRC
human pangenome reference, comparative pangenome references. These
are very high-stakes artifacts. Errors and biases propagate. "Get the
reference correct" is the operational form of "reliable, careful,
auditable," and it is the natural concrete demonstration anchor for
the spine.

## 3. DEMONSTRATION DESIGN

Longitudinal deployment of WorkGraph across the founders' active
genomics research programs (Erik's pangenomics and reference-building,
Luca's clinical genomics analysis). Real workflows, real outputs
(preprints, software releases, datasets), all coordinated through
auditable computation graphs. Vaughn produces ethnographic case
studies. BioBench is the open benchmark. Lab adoption is the
falsifiable external metric.

## 7. LIVERPOOL HIVE MIND POSITIONING

Liverpool's inaugural-cohort Hive Mind grant addresses hybrid coordination
for wet-lab autonomous chemistry. Poietic PBC addresses the parallel
architectural pattern for computational and clinical genomics. Different
domain, different coordination problem, complementary slot. Use this
framing in §28. Do not do independent web research.
