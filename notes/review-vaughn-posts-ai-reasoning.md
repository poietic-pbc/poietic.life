## Most Relevant Posts

### 1. Reasoning scaffolds: An infrastructure for human subjective reasoning

URL: <https://vaughntan.org/reasoningscaffolds>  
Date: 2025-10-22

Best fit for Poietic/WorkGraph. Vaughn argues that important decisions require subjective reasoning about objective facts: deciding what matters, why it matters, and how to persuade others. His "reasoning scaffolds" make that structure explicit so people can learn, inspect, and improve reasoning instead of treating good judgment as mysterious talent.

Landing-page-friendly theme:

> Vaughn designs ways to make human judgment visible, teachable, and auditable, so AI can support reasoning without pretending to replace it.

Why it matters for WorkGraph/Poietic:

- Matches WorkGraph's premise that complex work benefits from visible structure, explicit evaluation, and traceable reasoning.
- Supports Poietic's broader stance that AI tools should amplify human judgment, not blur where judgment comes from.
- Provides language for "scaffolding" as infrastructure rather than generic productivity tooling.

### 2. A better conceptual model for the AI agent

URL: <https://vaughntan.org/agentconstruct>  
Date: 2026-03-24

This is the most directly WorkGraph-adjacent post. Vaughn argues that treating "the agent" as the unit of improvement is too coarse. Instead, agents should be assembled from inspectable primitives: capabilities, success criteria, and trade-off configurations. Those parts can then be composed, evaluated, and evolved independently.

Landing-page-friendly theme:

> Vaughn treats AI agents less like opaque personas and more like modular work systems whose capabilities, goals, and trade-offs can be inspected and improved.

Why it matters for WorkGraph/Poietic:

- Strong conceptual overlap with task graphs, role decomposition, rubrics, evaluation traces, and composable agent behavior.
- Gives a principled reason to present WorkGraph as more than orchestration: it is a way to make AI work decomposable and improvable.
- Useful if the founder card wants a concrete AI-agent link rather than a broader human-reasoning link.

### 3. Prototyping a reasoning scaffold

URL: <https://vaughntan.org/protoscaffold>  
Date: 2025-10-06

This post makes the theory practical. Vaughn describes moving from paper prototypes and workshops to a functioning AI-supported tool that uses structured prompts and Socratic mirroring. The tool helps users build stronger arguments while keeping the meaning-making work with the user.

Landing-page-friendly theme:

> Vaughn builds practical tools that help people clarify their own reasoning, not outsource it.

Why it matters for WorkGraph/Poietic:

- Strong evidence that the reasoning-scaffold idea is not just conceptual; it has been tested across universities, corporations, startups, and government.
- Good support for founder-card language about "tool design for human judgment" or "AI tools for reasoning under uncertainty."
- Shows a product-development style compatible with Poietic: prototype the mechanism first, then scale the interface.

### 4. Designing AI tools that support critical thinking

URL: <https://vaughntan.org/aiux>  
Date: 2025-08-20

This post is the best UX/design articulation. Vaughn critiques empty prompt boxes because they make AI systems feel like meaning-making entities. His alternative is structured interaction that clarifies what the user must decide and what the machine can support.

Landing-page-friendly theme:

> Vaughn's AI design work separates what machines can process from what humans must judge.

Why it matters for WorkGraph/Poietic:

- Useful for positioning Poietic as a tool-builder with an explicit philosophy of human-machine division of labor.
- Connects critical thinking, interface design, and AI safety without sounding abstract.
- Helps avoid founder-card copy that overclaims AI autonomy.

### 5. The meaningmaking lens on AI (updated)

URL: <https://vaughntan.org/meaningmakingai>  
Date: 2025-09-28

This is a compact hub for Vaughn's AI/meaning-making series. It defines meaning-making as decisions about subjective value and collects the larger sequence of essays, including posts on AI expertise, education, critical thinking, tools, and agent design.

Landing-page-friendly theme:

> Vaughn's work asks where human meaning-making belongs in a world full of increasingly capable AI tools.

Why it matters for WorkGraph/Poietic:

- Best destination if the card wants one broad "read more" link rather than a single argument.
- Less sharply tied to WorkGraph than the reasoning-scaffold or agent-primitive posts, but more comprehensive.
- Useful as a fallback if the founder card needs a stable overview link.
