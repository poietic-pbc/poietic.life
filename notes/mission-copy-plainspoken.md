## Alternative 1

AI agents can already do useful work: search literature, write code, analyze data, and help with scientific design. The harder problem is coordination. People and machines need a way to share work over days or weeks, keep judgment visible, show evidence, and respond when the work changes.

Poietic builds open-source tools, public demos, and working methods for human-AI teams. The goal is work that can be reviewed, repeated, and understood. Our first product is [WorkGraph](https://graphwork.github.io/), a Rust task-coordination system where humans and AI agents share one dependency graph, with claims, logs, handoffs, artifacts, completions, and histories kept for review.

WorkGraph is our first instrument, not the whole project. It gives Poietic a way to test the [theory of organizational patterns](https://graphwork.github.io/theory/) in real work. Our public-benefit purpose is to make human-machine collaboration legible and responsive to the people in it: the work, the handoffs, the failures, and the evidence should be visible.

## Alternative 2

AI agents are becoming useful collaborators. They can search, draft, code, analyze, and test. What remains difficult is not just making them more capable, but helping people and agents work together over time: who did what, why it mattered, what evidence supports it, and when someone needs to respond.

Poietic builds open tools and methods for that kind of coordination. Our work includes products, live demonstrations, and organizational practice for hybrid human-AI teams. Our first product is [WorkGraph](https://graphwork.github.io/), a Rust system where humans and agents coordinate tasks in the same dependency graph and preserve claims, logs, handoffs, artifacts, decisions, and histories.

WorkGraph is the first product and test instrument for Poietic. It lets us develop the [theory of organizational patterns](https://graphwork.github.io/theory/) through real use, not only argument. As a public-benefit company, our aim is to make human-machine collaboration easier to inspect, easier to question, and more responsive to the people doing the work.

## Alternative 3

AI agents can now take on real parts of research and software work. They can find sources, write code, analyze results, and prepare designs. The hard part is keeping the collaboration organized: keeping track of evidence, judgment, handoffs, decisions, and accountability across more than one person or agent.

Poietic builds open-source products and practical methods for human-AI work. We want teams to see how work moved, what claims were made, what evidence was used, and where a person needs to step in. [WorkGraph](https://graphwork.github.io/) is our first product: a Rust task-coordination system where humans and AI agents work in one dependency graph with durable logs and artifacts.

WorkGraph is one instrument inside a larger public-benefit effort. It is how Poietic tests its [theory of organizational patterns](https://graphwork.github.io/theory/) in live work. The goal is simple: make human-machine collaboration legible, reviewable, and responsive to the participants, including when the work fails or changes direction.
