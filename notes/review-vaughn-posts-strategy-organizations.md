## Most Relevant Posts

### 1. A better conceptual model for the AI agent

URL: https://vaughntan.org/agentconstruct  
Date: 2026-03-24

This is the strongest direct fit for WorkGraph. Tan argues that AI agents should not be treated as monolithic workers; they should be composed from smaller, inspectable primitives: capabilities, success criteria, and decision rules. He grounds the claim in organization theory: people in high-uncertainty teams are not monoliths either, and effective organizations change role configurations without throwing away the whole person or team.

Landing-page-friendly theme: WorkGraph makes hybrid work reliable by decomposing agents, tasks, and evaluation criteria into parts that humans can inspect, recombine, and improve.

Why it matters for Poietic: This maps tightly to WorkGraph's task graph, agent roles, rubrics, and evaluation loops. The post also distinguishes individual agent memory from organizational memory, which is central to WorkGraph's value proposition as a durable coordination substrate.

### 2. Decomposable strategy problems

URL: https://vaughntan.org/decomposableproblems  
Date: 2025-06-02

Tan breaks a large public-sector utility problem into five different problem types: coordination, strategy framework, category, capability, and tradeoff. The core move is not "solve the whole hard thing"; it is to make the problem typed and tractable, then find the highest-leverage place to intervene. In his example, surfacing tradeoffs gives stakeholders a practical way to unblock the rest.

Landing-page-friendly theme: The hardest organizational problems become workable when teams can separate coordination, capability, uncertainty, and tradeoff problems instead of treating them as one vague mess.

Why it matters for Poietic: This is almost exactly the work WorkGraph does operationally. It turns amorphous collaborative work into typed tasks with dependencies, owners, acceptance criteria, and review paths.

### 3. Reasoning scaffolds: An infrastructure for human subjective reasoning

URL: https://vaughntan.org/reasoningscaffolds  
Date: 2025-10-22

Tan describes reasoning scaffolds as explicit structures for making subjective reasoning visible, teachable, and auditable. The post emphasizes the distinction between objective facts and subjective judgments about what matters, why it matters, and what should be prioritized. AI can support information organization, but the value judgment must remain visible as human work.

Landing-page-friendly theme: Poietic keeps human judgment in the loop by making the reasoning structure explicit instead of hiding value judgments inside fluent AI output.

Why it matters for Poietic: This supports the current founder-card sentence about "keeping human judgment explicit." It also gives a more precise language for WorkGraph's rubrics, review steps, and audit trails: not just provenance of actions, but provenance of judgment.

### 4. Good strategy must focus on tradeoffs, not just goals

URL: https://vaughntan.org/strattradeoffs  
Date: 2025-05-01

Tan argues that shared goals are often weak alignment signals because teams can agree on goals while making incompatible sacrifices. The useful alignment work is explicit agreement on tradeoffs: what desirable outcomes each unit is willing to give up to pursue something more important.

Landing-page-friendly theme: WorkGraph helps teams coordinate autonomous action by making tradeoffs explicit before they become execution conflicts.

Why it matters for Poietic: This is useful for explaining why WorkGraph needs rubrics, acceptance criteria, and evaluation agents. Coordination fails when hidden tradeoffs are discovered only after work ships.

### 5. Content, representation, re-presentation

URL: https://vaughntan.org/re-presentation  
Date: 2026-03-28

Tan distinguishes source documents from product documents. LLMs make it cheap to transform a rich source into many audience-specific outputs, but the quality bottleneck moves to source quality and human evaluation. He argues source material should capture reasoning, distinguish observation from interpretation, mark claim status, and preserve context so generated outputs can be checked.

Landing-page-friendly theme: WorkGraph treats the work trace as the durable source layer, so summaries, reports, and decisions can be regenerated without losing the reasoning behind them.

Why it matters for Poietic: This is a strong bridge to WorkGraph's public computation graphs and auditability claims. It explains why the graph is more than a project log: it is structured source material for future synthesis and evaluation.
